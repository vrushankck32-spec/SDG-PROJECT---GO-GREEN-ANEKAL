Our Solutions

SDGs Addressed by Go Green Anekal

Go Green Anekal collaborates with corporates, institutions, and organizations to implement CSR-funded sustainability initiatives centered on floriculture. The model integrates community participation, digital monitoring, and export-linked value creation.

Corporate employees sponsor flower cultivation plots in selected villages. Local women monitor these plots using a QR-enabled tracking system. Harvested flowers are consolidated and exported, generating environmental benefits, rural employment, and measurable economic value.

Sustainability Alignment

How Many SDGs Are Addressed?

Go Green Anekal directly or indirectly contributes to 7 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), combining livelihood creation, gender equity, climate action, and cross-sector partnerships.

SDG Impact Breakdown

Detailed SDG Contributions

SDG 1 – No Poverty

Sponsored flower plots generate predictable income for rural households. Export-linked pricing ensures higher earnings than local markets and reduces dependence on seasonal or informal labor.

Impact Type: Direct
SDG 5 – Gender Equality

Local women are trained and paid as plot monitors. QR-based digital tracking builds technical confidence and moves women into supervisory and data-linked roles.

Impact Type: Direct
SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth

The initiative formalizes rural employment in floriculture, links villages to global export markets, and promotes skill development in agri-tech, quality control, and logistics.

Impact Type: Direct
SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure

QR-enabled monitoring introduces digital agricultural infrastructure. Traceability improves transparency for corporate sponsors and enables a scalable CSR–tech–agriculture model.

Impact Type: Direct
SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production

Controlled cultivation reduces waste and chemical overuse. Traceable plots improve accountability and export-grade efficiency enhances overall resource utilization.

Impact Type: Direct
SDG 13 – Climate Action

Floriculture increases green cover, improves water efficiency through polyhouse cultivation, and reduces land degradation compared to traditional farming practices.

Impact Type: Indirect but Measurable
SDG 15 – Life on Land

Sustainable land use prevents soil exhaustion common in mono-cropping and promotes biodiversity-friendly cultivation methods.

Impact Type: Indirect
SDG 17 – Partnerships for the Goals

Corporates fund CSR initiatives, communities implement and maintain operations, and export buyers complete the value chain through long-term partnerships.

Impact Type: Structural / Enabling
