The Problem

Rural Sustainability Gaps

Rural regions like Anekal face structural challenges that limit inclusive growth and transparent environmental action.

Women lack access to stable livelihood opportunities, agricultural land remains underutilized, and CSR-led environmental initiatives often operate without real-time monitoring or measurable community impact. At the same time, corporate organizations struggle to translate CSR investments into verifiable outcomes.

Our Solution

A Measurable CSR Ecosystem

Go Green Anekal bridges corporate CSR goals with rural empowerment through technology-enabled floriculture.

Go Green Anekal collaborates with corporates,institutions, and organizations to implement CSR-funded sustainability initiatives centered on floriculture. Corporate employees participate by sponsoring flower cultivation plots in selected villages. Local women monitor these plots using a QR-enabled tracking system. The harvested flowers are then consolidated and exported, generating environmental benefits, rural employment, and measurable economic value.
